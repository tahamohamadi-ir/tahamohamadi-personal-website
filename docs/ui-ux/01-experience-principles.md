# 01 — Experience principles

**UX-PRINCIPLE-001 — Purpose.** Present credible bilingual academic, professional, and technical profile evidence so visitors can understand research, experience, writing, projects, publications, and contact paths without marketing spectacle.

| Audience | Primary outcome |
|---|---|
| Academic reviewer | Assess research fit, publications, and resume evidence. |
| Recruiter or employer | Scan skills, experience, and project evidence. |
| Technical reader | Read structured writing and technical work. |
| General visitor | Identify person and reach contact. |

**UX-PRINCIPLE-002 — Content first.** Prioritize identity, supported evidence, readable content, and contact over promotion. CMS absence is explicit; factual claims are never inferred from layout or placeholders.

**UX-PRINCIPLE-003 — Equal locale experience.** Persian RTL and English LTR are equal first-class public experiences. Locale availability, alternates, bidi treatment, and recovery are owned by [05](05-content-and-localization.md).

**UX-PRINCIPLE-004 — Editorial restraint.** Academic Editorial Utility is approved public direction. Foundations, visual prohibitions, and component hierarchy are owned by [design-system/foundations.md](design-system/foundations.md), [design-system/anti-patterns.md](design-system/anti-patterns.md), and [design-system/components.md](design-system/components.md).
